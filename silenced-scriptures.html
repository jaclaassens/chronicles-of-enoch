Chapter One

The Blessing of Enoch

ቃለ ፡ በረከት ፡ ዘሄኖክ

Qala barakat za-Henok — "Words of blessing of Enoch"

Verse 1

"The words of the blessing of Enoch, according to which he blessed the chosen and righteous, who must be present on the Day of Distress, which is appointed, for the removal of all the wicked and impious."

"Die woorde van die seen van Henok, waarmee hy die uitverkorenes en regverdiges geseen het, wat teenwoordig moet wees op die Dag van Benoudheid, wat bestem is vir die verwydering van al die goddeloses en oneerbiediges."

Reveal the frequencies +
Linguistic

"Blessing" — ברכה (berakah)
Ge'ez: በረከት (barakat) | Hebrew root: ברך (b-r-k)
This is the same root as in "baruch" (blessed). The blessing is not passive — it is an ACT, a transfer of spiritual power.
Compare: Genesis 1:28 — God BLESSED them (וַיְבָרֶךְ)

Frequency

"Chosen and righteous"
Ge'ez: ኅሩያን ወጻድቃን (heruyaan wa-tsaadqaan)
Two categories, not one:
• ኅሩያን (heruyaan) = chosen/elect — SELECTED by God
• ጻድቃን (tsaadqaan) = righteous — ALIGNED with God
Being chosen and being righteous are SEPARATE but connected.

Hebrew

"Day of Distress"
Ge'ez: ዕለተ ፡ ምንዳቤ (elata mendaabe)
Compare: יוֹם צָרָה (yom tsarah) — "day of trouble/distress"
Used in: Psalm 20:1, Zephaniah 1:15, Jeremiah 16:19
This is the YOM — the DAY — a specific appointed time. Not vague. DESTINED.

The Silence

The opening verse makes clear: This book is about JUDGMENT.
Not generic spirituality — SEPARATION.
The Silence wants unity without distinction.
Enoch starts with: There are TWO kinds of people.

Question

Why does Enoch begin with BLESSING, not warning?
The blessing comes BEFORE the description of judgment.
The frequency is established FIRST — then the contrast is shown.

Verse 2

"And Enoch took up his parable, and said: There was a righteous man, whose eyes were opened by the Lord, and he saw a Holy vision in the Heavens, which the Angels showed to me, and I heard everything from them. And I understood what I saw, but not for this generation, but for a distant generation that will come."

"En Henok het sy gelykenis opgeneem en gese: Daar was 'n regverdige man, wie se oe deur die Here geopen is, en hy het 'n Heilige visioen in die Hemele gesien, wat die Engele aan my getoon het, en ek het alles van hulle gehoor. En ek het verstaan wat ek gesien het, maar nie vir hierdie geslag nie, maar vir 'n verre geslag wat sal kom."

Reveal the frequencies +
Linguistic

"Parable" — משל (mashal)
Ge'ez: ምሳሌ (mesaale) | Hebrew: מָשָׁל (mashal)
NOT just a "story" — a mashal is: a proverb, a prophetic utterance, a wisdom saying with hidden meaning, a comparison revealing truth.
Enoch "lifts up" (נָשָׂא / nasa) his mashal — he ELEVATES it. This is prophetic speech, not casual writing.

Frequency

"Eyes were opened"
Ge'ez: ተከፈታ ፡ አዕይንቲሁ (takaftaa aeyentiihu)
The passive voice: his eyes WERE OPENED — by the Lord. He did not open them himself.
This is REVELATION — uncovering what is already there.
Compare: Genesis 3:7 — "the eyes of both were opened" (after the fall)
Compare: 2 Kings 6:17 — Elisha prays for servant's eyes to be opened
The capacity to SEE is given, not earned.

Hebrew

"The Lord"
Ge'ez: እግዚአብሔር (Egzi'abher) = "Lord of the Universe"
This is the Ethiopian equivalent of "LORD" — but is it YHWH?
In Ethiopian tradition, this title IS the Name.
Question: Did the original Aramaic/Hebrew have יהוה (YHWH)?

The Silence

"Not for this generation"
Enoch explicitly states: This is NOT for his contemporaries.
It is for a "DISTANT generation that will come."
The Silence wants to keep this "historical" — safely in the past.
But Enoch says it's for THE FUTURE.
Are WE that distant generation?

Compare

Shifts from third to first person
"There was a righteous man" (third person)
"which the Angels showed TO ME" (first person)
This is not an error — it's a literary device. Enoch speaks of himself as if from outside, then enters the narrative. The reader is pulled from observer to participant.

Verse 3

"Concerning the chosen, I spoke, and I uttered a parable concerning them: The Holy and Great One will come out from His dwelling."

"Aangaande die uitverkorenes het ek gepraat, en ek het 'n gelykenis oor hulle uitgespreek: Die Heilige en Grote Een sal uit Sy woning uitgaan."

Reveal the frequencies +
Linguistic

"The Holy and Great One"
Ge'ez: ቅዱስ ፡ ወዐቢይ (Qeddus wa-Abiiy)
ቅዱስ (Qeddus) = Holy — set apart, sacred
ወዐቢይ (wa-Abiiy) = and Great — mighty, vast
Hebrew equivalent: הַקָּדוֹשׁ הַגָּדוֹל (HaKadosh HaGadol)
This is a TITLE, not the Name. Compare: "The Holy One of Israel" in Isaiah.

Frequency

"Will come out from His dwelling"
Ge'ez: ይወፅእ ፡ እምነ ፡ ማኅደሩ (yewatse' emna maakhdaru)
The Holy One does not STAY in heaven — He COMES OUT.
His dwelling (מָעוֹן / ma'on) is not His prison.
This is the pattern: God MOVES toward His creation.
The Incarnation is foreshadowed here.

The Silence

The Silence wants God distant, abstract, impersonal.
Enoch says: He will COME OUT.

Verse 4

"And the Eternal God will tread, from there, upon Mount Sinai, and He will appear with His Host, and will appear in the strength of His Power from Heaven."

"En die Ewige God sal van daar af op die Berg Sinai trap, en Hy sal met Sy Leerskare verskyn, en sal verskyn in die krag van Sy Mag uit die Hemel."

Reveal the frequencies +
Linguistic

"Eternal God"
Ge'ez: አምላከ ፡ ዓለም (Amlaaka aalam)
Literally: "God of the Age/World/Eternity"
Hebrew: אֵל עוֹלָם (El Olam) — "Everlasting God"
Used in Genesis 21:33 — Abraham planted a tree and called on El Olam.
This is one of the divine NAMES, not just a title.

Frequency

"Will tread upon Mount Sinai"
The verb ירגז (ragaz) usually means "to tremble" or "cause to tremble" — His FOOTSTEPS cause trembling.
Mount Sinai — the place of the Torah's giving.
The same mountain will see His RETURN.
Pattern: Where law was given, judgment will come.

Compare

Deuteronomy 33:2
"YHWH came from Sinai, and dawned from Seir upon them; he shone forth from Mount Paran; he came from the ten thousands of holy ones, with flaming fire at his right hand."
Jude 1:14-15 QUOTES 1 Enoch 1:9 — this passage is in Scripture.
The New Testament validates Enoch's prophecy.

Hebrew

"His Host" — צבא (tsaba)
Ge'ez: ሰራዊቱ (sarawiitu)
The heavenly army — angels, holy ones, the divine council.
Not alone — with TEN THOUSANDS (see verse 9).
This is not a private appearance — it's a cosmic invasion.

Verse 5

"And all shall be smitten with fear, and the Watchers shall quake, and great fear and trembling shall seize them unto the ends of the earth."

"En almal sal met vrees geslaan word, en die Watchers sal bewe, en groot vrees en bewing sal hulle aangryp tot aan die eindes van die aarde."

Reveal the frequencies +
Linguistic

"Watchers" — עִירִין (Irin)
Ge'ez: ትጉሓን (Teguhaan)
From root: ትጉህ (teguh) = "awake, vigilant"
Aramaic: עִיר (Ir) = "watcher, wakeful one"
This is the FIRST mention of the Watchers in the text.
They are introduced in the context of FEAR.

Frequency

The Watchers FEAR
The Watchers are not just any angels. They are the ones who FELL (chapter 6 onwards).
Even THEY — who rebelled — will tremble.
This is not selective judgment — it reaches THEM.
The ends of the earth AND the fallen angels.

The Silence

The Silence removed this book partly because it NAMES the Watchers.
It provides too much detail about WHO rebelled and HOW.
Keeping it vague serves the Silence.
Enoch is specific.

Verse 6

"And the high mountains shall be shaken, and the high hills shall be made low, and shall melt like wax before the flame."

"En die hoe berge sal geskud word, en die hoe heuwels sal laag gemaak word, en sal smelt soos was voor die vlam."

Reveal the frequencies +
Compare

Micah 1:4 — "And the mountains will melt under him, and the valleys will split open, like wax before the fire."

Psalm 97:5 — "The mountains melt like wax before YHWH, before the Lord of all the earth."

Frequency

Mountains = Powers
In prophetic literature, mountains often symbolize: Kingdoms, Powers, Proud structures, Things that seem immovable.
The "high hills made low" — this is LEVELING.
What seems permanent will dissolve.

Linguistic

"Like wax before flame"
Ge'ez: ከመ ፡ ሰዐ (kama sa'a) = "like wax"
The image is TOTAL dissolution. Not damaged — MELTED.
What seemed solid becomes liquid, formless.

Verse 7

"And the earth shall be wholly rent in sunder, and all that is upon the earth shall perish, and there shall be a judgement upon all."

"En die aarde sal heeltemal uitmekaar geskeur word, en alles wat op die aarde is sal vergaan, en daar sal 'n oordeel oor almal wees."

Reveal the frequencies +
Frequency

"Judgement upon ALL"
Ge'ez: ኵሉ (kwellu) = ALL, everyone
Not some. Not the especially wicked. ALL.
Universal judgment — then distinction (verse 8).
The sequence: ALL face judgment, THEN separation.

Question

Is this the Flood (Noah's time)? Or the final judgment (end times)?
Or BOTH — a pattern that repeats?
Enoch seems to collapse time — what happened, what will happen.

Verse 8

"But with the righteous He will make peace, and will protect the elect, and mercy shall be upon them. And they shall all belong to God, and they shall be prospered, and they shall all be blessed. And He will help them all, and light shall appear unto them, and He will make peace with them."

"Maar met die regverdiges sal Hy vrede maak, en Hy sal die uitverkorenes beskerm, en genade sal op hulle wees. En hulle sal almal aan God behoort, en hulle sal voorspoedig wees, en hulle sal almal geseen word. En Hy sal hulle almal help, en lig sal aan hulle verskyn, en Hy sal vrede met hulle maak."

Reveal the frequencies +
Frequency

"Light shall appear"
Ge'ez: ብርሃን (berhaan) = light
The righteous receive LIGHT. Not just protection — ILLUMINATION.
Compare: John 1:4-5 — "In Him was life, and the life was the light of men."
The frequency of the righteous is LIGHT.

Linguistic

"Peace" — שָׁלוֹם (shalom)
Ge'ez: ሰላም (salaam)
Appears THREE times in this verse:
1. "make peace" | 2. "mercy upon them" | 3. "make peace with them"
Triple emphasis — complete peace.
שָׁלוֹם is not just absence of conflict — it is WHOLENESS.

Compare

The Contrast
Verse 7: Judgment on ALL
Verse 8: Peace for the righteous
The contrast is stark. No middle ground.
This is the FREQUENCY SEPARATION.
Same event, two outcomes, based on alignment.

Verse 9

"And behold! He comes with ten thousand of His holy ones, to execute judgment upon all, and to destroy all the ungodly, and to convict all flesh of all the works of their ungodliness which they have ungodly committed, and of all the hard things which ungodly sinners have spoken against Him."

"En kyk! Hy kom met tien duisend van Sy heiliges, om oordeel oor almal uit te voer, en om al die goddeloses te vernietig, en om alle vlees te oortuig van al die werke van hulle goddeloosheid wat hulle goddeloos gepleeg het, en van al die harde dinge wat goddelose sondaars teen Hom gespreek het."

Compare

JUDE 1:14-15 QUOTES THIS VERSE
"Enoch, the seventh from Adam, prophesied about them: 'See, the Lord is coming with thousands upon thousands of his holy ones to judge everyone, and to convict all of them of all the ungodly acts they have committed in their ungodliness, and of all the defiant words ungodly sinners have spoken against him.'"

This is SCRIPTURE quoting ENOCH.
The New Testament validates this prophecy.
The book was removed, but the quote remains.

Frequency

"Ten thousand holy ones"
Ge'ez: እልፍ ፡ ቅዱሳን (elf qeddusaan) = "ten thousand holy ones"
Hebrew: רִבְבוֹת קֹדֶשׁ (ribb'vot qodesh)
Compare Deuteronomy 33:2 — "ten thousands of holy ones"
The number is not literal — it means INNUMERABLE.
The holy ones are the divine army.

Linguistic

"Ungodly" repeated FOUR times
This is not poor writing — it's emphasis.
• ungodly (people)
• ungodliness (state)
• ungodly committed (action)
• ungodly sinners (identity)
The word saturates the sentence.
It describes complete corruption — being, state, action, identity.

The Silence

This verse is WHY the book was removed.
It's TOO clear. It names judgment. It names the method. It names the outcome.
The Silence prefers ambiguity.
Enoch is precise.

Chapter 1 Summary — The Opening Pattern

  • Blessing first (v.1) — The righteous are blessed BEFORE judgment described
  • Eyes opened (v.2) — Revelation requires divine intervention
  • For a distant generation (v.2) — This is for THE FUTURE
  • God will COME OUT (v.3-4) — He is not distant
  • Watchers will fear (v.5) — Even the fallen angels tremble
  • Creation dissolves (v.6-7) — The old order cannot stand
  • Two outcomes (v.7-8) — Judgment on all, peace for the righteous
  • Scripture quotes this (v.9) — Jude validates Enoch
