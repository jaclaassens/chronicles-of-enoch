World-Building

The Cosmology

"The earth was formless and void, and darkness was over the surface of the deep." But what shape did God give it? And did that shape survive the flood?

The Pre-Flood World

A flat disc beneath the firmament, suspended on nothing

The Biblical Foundation

Job 26:7 says God "hangs the earth on nothing." The Hebrew word for earth here is erets—not a sphere, but land, territory, ground. Something suspended, yes. But suspended how?

Genesis describes waters above the firmament and waters below. The firmament (raqia) is something beaten out, spread thin, like hammered metal. A dome. A barrier.

In Our Story

Before the flood, the world was a flat disc—the "hanging stone" of Job. The firmament arched above, holding back the waters of the heavens. The sun, moon, and stars moved within this dome, not beyond it.

"They think they know the shape of the world. They are wrong. The world they know is a wound that hasn't finished healing."

The Post-Flood World

God didn't just flood the world. He remade it.

The Greater Earth

What we call "the world" is merely the Inner World—the known continents enclosed by the Ice Wall. But beyond the wall? Realms that survived the flood because they were never part of the original judgment.

  • Asgard — Where the Norse "gods" (Nephilim) fled
  • Eden — Still guarded, still growing the Tree of Life
  • Atlantis — Where Azazel escaped to (later destroyed)
  • Lemuria — The Sound Builders and their crystal towers
  • Mu, Aten, Hyperborea — Other realms, other survivors

The Waters Below

Inside the sphere of the Greater Earth, accessible only through frequency barriers, lies an uncorrupted world—the Waters Below. A world waiting to be found. A world that never fell.

Power Systems

Sorcerers vs Warlocks

At the gnats, Pharaoh's magicians failed. "This is the finger of God," they said. Why gnats? Because stolen power is finite. You can only take what exists. But creating life from dust? That requires the Source.

Sorcerers: Those Who Channel

Ask. Receive. Conduct.

The Method

Sorcerers do not generate power—they channel it. The Source frequency flows through them but is not of them. They are instruments, not batteries.

  • Power comes through prayer, alignment, surrender
  • The frequency passes through; it does not stay
  • Unlimited potential—limited only by faith and obedience
  • Cannot be depleted because it's not theirs to deplete

Biblical Examples

  • Moses — Staff became serpent, parted the sea, struck the rock
  • Elijah — Called fire from heaven, raised the dead
  • Enoch — Walked with God, never died
  • Jesus — "I only do what I see the Father doing"

Warlocks: Those Who Steal

Take. Steal. Consume.

The Method

Warlocks cannot create—they can only corrupt. They are frequency vampires, stealing power from existing sources: blood, fear, death, forbidden contracts.

  • Power comes through taking, not asking
  • Energy is stored, hoarded, depleted
  • Limited by what can be stolen
  • Eventually runs out—requires constant feeding

Why the Gnats?

Pharaoh's magicians matched Moses plague for plague—until the gnats. They could transform existing matter (staffs to serpents, water to blood). But creating life from dust? That requires generating, not stealing.

"This is the finger of God." — Exodus 8:19

Examples

  • Cain's line — The first murderer, the first to take life for power
  • Nimrod — The mighty hunter, consuming souls
  • Pharaoh's magicians — Matched, then failed
  • The Witch of Endor — Trading in stolen frequencies

The Watchers

"And it came to pass, when the children of men had multiplied, that in those days were born unto them beautiful and comely daughters. And the angels, the children of the heaven, saw and lusted after them..." — 1 Enoch 6:1-2

Semjaza Leader of the 200

"He Who Sees the Name" — Supreme Commander of the Watchers

He organized the rebellion but made all 200 share the guilt equally. When they hesitated, he bound them with an oath on Mount Hermon: "Let us all swear together, and bind ourselves by mutual curses."

Click to expand

Etymology

Semjaza (שמיחזה) — From "shem" (name) + "chazah" (to see). "My name has seen" or "He sees the Name." He saw God's true name—and still chose rebellion.

What He Taught

  • Root-cuttings and plant magic (pharmakeia)
  • Enchantments bound to living things
  • The secrets the Earth keeps in her roots

His Fate

Bound for seventy generations in the valleys of the earth, awaiting the Day of Judgment. Jewish folklore places him as the constellation Orion—hanging eternally in the sky, repentant but unforgiven.

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 6:3-7, 8:3, 10:11-15; Book of Giants; Talmud Yoma 67b

Azazel Chief Watcher

"Scapegoat of God" — Teacher of Forbidden Arts

He taught humanity the arts of war and vanity—swords and cosmetics, weapons and jewelry. The blame for corruption fell heaviest on him. Creation itself rejects his presence.

Click to expand

Etymology

Azazel (עזאזל) — Possibly "Fierce God" from "azaz" (fierce/strong) + "el" (God), or "Goat that departs" connecting to the Yom Kippur ritual.

What He Taught

  • Metalworking: swords, knives, shields, breastplates
  • Cosmetics: antimony (kohl), beautifying of eyelids, dyeing
  • Jewelry and mirrors—instruments of vanity
  • "The whole earth has been corrupted through the works taught by Azazel"

His Fate

Supposed to be bound in Dudael—a pit in the wilderness covered with jagged rocks, awaiting the lake of fire. But in our story, he fled to Atlantis. He avoided Dudael but cannot avoid creation's rejection.

In Our Story

Azazel escaped the flood by fleeing to the outer realms. He cannot be destroyed, but he can be made irrelevant. Creation itself is slowly marginalizing him—the frequency cannot tolerate his anti-frequency presence.

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 8:1-2, 9:6, 10:4-8; Leviticus 16:8-10 (scapegoat ritual)

Sariel Translated Watcher

"Prince of God" — The One Who Was Saved

She appears in both "fallen" and "righteous" angel lists. In our story, she was translated— saved before the flood. Love has a way of preserving what should be lost.

Click to expand

Etymology

Sariel (שריאל) — From "sar" (prince/commander) + "el" (God). "Prince of God" or "Command of God."

What She Taught

  • The course of the moon and lunar cycles
  • Calendar knowledge and time-keeping
  • Women's mysteries connected to the moon

The Dual Tradition

Sariel appears as both a fallen Watcher (1 Enoch 6:7) AND as one of the holy angels set over spirits (1 Enoch 20:6). She was both—and that's the point. Redemption changed her list.

In Our Story

She walks freely between Eden and the world. She loved Enoch's grandfather—and that love preserved her. She knows "the door that is not a door." Her lunar knowledge gives her domain over cycles, time, and frequency access.

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3, 9:1, 20:6; Book of Jubilees; Dead Sea Scrolls War Scroll

Baraqel Chief Watcher

"Lightning of God" — Father of Mahaway

He taught humanity to read the storms. His son Mahaway became one of the giants condemned to destruction—and dreamed of his father's lightning until the end.

Click to expand

Etymology

Baraqel (ברקאל) — From "baraq" (lightning) + "el" (God). "Lightning of God."

What He Taught

  • Astrology and the signs of lightning
  • Weather divination and storm-reading
  • The voices hidden in the thunder

The Norse Connection

The Asgardians remember lightning-wielders. Does Thor carry Baraqel's frequency? Is thunder the echo of a Watcher's teaching, preserved beyond the Ice Wall?

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3; Book of Giants 4Q530, 4Q531 (Mahaway called "son of Baraq'el")

Penemue Associated Leader

"Inside of God" — Teacher of Writing

He taught humanity to write. "Through this many have erred from eternity to eternity." The Book of Enoch condemning the Watchers exists because Penemue gave humanity letters.

Click to expand

Etymology

Penemue (פנמואל) — From "panim" (face/inside) + "el" (God). "Inside of God" or "Face of God."

What He Taught

  • Writing and literacy
  • The making of paper and ink
  • The secrets of wisdom made permanent

The Irony

Enoch the scribe used corrupted knowledge to condemn the corruptors. Every word he wrote—including the judgment against the Watchers—exists because Penemue taught writing. Can the frequency redeem even Watcher-teaching?

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 69:8-11

Gadreel Ancient Watcher

"Wall of God" — The First Tempter

Before the 200 descended on Hermon, Gadreel had already found Eve in Eden. The wall that should have protected became the breach. The Silence's first tool.

Click to expand

Etymology

Gadreel (גדראל) — From "gader" (wall/fence) + "el" (God). "Wall of God." A name of protection turned ironic.

What He Taught

  • Led Eve astray (associated with the serpent)
  • "All the blows of death" — the knowledge of mortality
  • He showed humanity death before they were meant to know it

The Garden Connection

This connects the Watcher narrative directly to Genesis 3. The Silence worked through Gadreel before the Watchers formally fell. The corruption began in Paradise, before Mount Hermon, before the oath.

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 69:6-7

Kokabiel Chief Watcher

"Star of God" — The Star-Mapper

He gave humanity the constellations and taught them to read fate in the stars. The Atenists still honor his teachings—they met him before the corruption.

Click to expand

Etymology

Kokabiel (כוכבאל) — From "kokav" (star) + "el" (God). "Star of God."

What He Taught

  • The constellations and their names
  • Zodiacal astrology
  • Navigation by starlight

In Our Story

The Land of Aten—the Sun Disc—remembers the Watchers as teachers. They "mapped the firmament when humanity still lived in caves." Kokabiel's stellar knowledge survives in their astronomy.

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3; Book of Raziel

Armaros Chief Watcher

"The Cursed One" — Breaker of Spells

While his brothers taught curses, Armaros taught how to break them. Was he trying to help? Or did he merely create dependency—humanity needing him to undo what the others did?

Click to expand

Etymology

Armaros (ארמרוס) — Possibly from "cherem" (devoted to destruction/cursed) or "One from Hermon."

What He Taught

  • The resolving of enchantments (counter-spells)
  • How to break magical bonds
  • Protections against other Watchers' teachings

In Our Story

His knowledge is what Sorcerers use to resist Warlock power. The frequency that undoes corruption—did it come from a fallen angel trying to help? Or from one playing both sides?

Apocryphal Sources

1 Enoch 6:7, 8:3

Encyclopedia

The Norse "Gods"

They weren't gods. They were survivors—Nephilim and Watchers who fled beyond the Ice Wall before the flood swept the Inner World clean. Their myths are memories. Their powers are remnants. Their grief is eternal.

Odin All-Father

"Fury, Ecstasy, Inspiration" — Ruler of the Asgardian Council

He sacrificed one eye at Mimir's Well for wisdom. He sees more with one than others see with two. He organized the exodus over the Ice Wall and became "All-Father" because he gathered the refugees.

Click to expand

Etymology

Odin (Oðinn) — From Old Norse "odr" meaning fury, ecstasy, or inspiration. He is the god of that divine madness that possesses poets and warriors alike.

His Artifacts

  • Gungnir — Spear that never misses, runes carved on its tip (frequency weapon?)
  • Huginn & Muninn — Ravens: Thought and Memory. Bound spirits? Corrupted cherubim?
  • Geri & Freki — Wolves: Greedy and Voracious. Perhaps Watchers reduced to beast-form
  • Sleipnir — Eight-legged horse, born of Loki. Traverses all nine realms

The Sacrifice

He hung on Yggdrasil for nine nights, pierced by his own spear, to discover the runes— corrupted angelic script that encodes the frequencies of creation. He traded an eye for wisdom at Mimir's Well. His pursuit of knowledge echoes the Watchers' hunger.

In Our Story

He knows Ragnarok is coming. He schemes to delay it. He knows the Flood happened once and fears it will happen again. His voice speaks in riddles and kennings, never giving straight answers, weighted with ancient weariness.

Thor The Thunderer

"Thunder" — Young, Passionate, Burdened with Guilt

Young for a Nephilim—barely 300 years old at the Flood. He wanted to save the drowning. He carries guilt for not crossing the Wall. His thunder rattles Valhalla's foundations when he remembers what they let happen.

Click to expand

Etymology

Thor (Thunraz) — Simply means "Thunder." The elemental force personified. Second or third generation Nephilim.

His Artifacts

  • Mjolnir — "Crusher." Short handle (Loki's sabotage), always returns, only he can lift it
  • Megingjord — Belt that doubles his strength. A frequency amplifier?
  • Jarngreipr — Iron gloves required to wield Mjolnir. Insulation against the power
  • Tanngrisnir & Tanngnjostr — Goats that can be killed, eaten, resurrected if bones intact

The Watcher Connection

Does Thor carry Baraqel's frequency? The Lightning Watcher taught storm-signs. Thor wields lightning. Is the thunder of Asgard an echo of Watcher teaching, preserved beyond the Ice Wall?

In Our Story

Direct, honest, quick to anger, quick to forgive, fiercely loyal. He wanted to help during the Flood. Odin held him back. That moment broke something in him.

Freya The Golden Lady

"Lady" — Ancient Beyond Imagining

She has lived in Asgard since BEFORE the Watchers fell. She may be from an earlier, unfallen line—or among the very first Watchers. She taught Odin seidr: frequency manipulation through singing and chanting.

Click to expand

Etymology

Freya (Freyja) — Simply means "Lady." A title, not a name. What was she called before she became the Lady?

Her Artifacts

  • Brisingamen — Necklace obtained from four dwarves for four nights. What did she really trade?
  • Valhamr — Falcon cloak that allows flight between realms
  • Chariot pulled by two large cats

Goddess of Death

She is goddess of love, beauty, war—and DEATH. She chooses half of the slain warriors (Odin gets the other half). She weeps tears of red gold for her lost husband Od. Seductive but dangerous. Pragmatic about survival.

In Our Story

Her voice speaks in poetry when moved. She taught Odin seidr—frequency manipulation through song. She remembers things the other Asgardians have forgotten. Or pretends to forget.

Frigg The Queen

"Beloved" — She Who Knows All Fates

Odin's wife and equal in knowledge. She KNOWS all fates but speaks of none. Her silence is discipline, not ignorance. Her foreknowledge is her burden—she sees the ends and loves anyway.

Click to expand

Etymology

Frigg (Frijjo) — From "beloved." She is love itself made flesh, the bond that holds Asgard together.

Her Burden

She spins the clouds with her distaff—weaving fate itself. She made all things swear not to harm Baldur. All things except the mistletoe, too young to swear. She knew. She loved anyway.

In Our Story

Measured, careful, says less than she knows, warm with those she trusts. She knows how Ragnarok ends. She knows what Baldur's death means. She knows—and her silence is love, not cruelty.

Tyr The One-Handed

"THE God" — Once the Supreme Deity

His name literally means "God"—he was once the supreme deity before Odin rose. He lost his right hand binding Fenrir. He knew the gods were lying to the wolf. He paid the price alone.

Click to expand

Etymology

Tyr (Tiwaz) — Literally means "THE God." The same root as Latin "deus," Greek "Zeus." He was THE god before the others rose.

The Sacrifice

The gods bound Fenrir with a magical chain, promising to release him. A lie. Tyr put his hand in Fenrir's mouth as surety. When Fenrir realized the betrayal, he bit. Tyr knew it would happen. He paid the price for Asgard's deception.

His End

At Ragnarok, he kills and is killed by Garm, the hound of Hel. Justice served and received. Perhaps a half-giant himself, torn between worlds like the Nephilim.

In Our Story

Few words, all precise. Judgmental but fair. He will look Odin in the eye and tell him he's wrong. The god of honorable war—in a family that has forgotten honor.

Baldur The Shining One

"Bold, Brave" or "Shining" — The Most Uncorrupted

He radiates actual light—his frequency is so pure it becomes visible. The most uncorrupted of the Nephilim. His death is the moment Asgard realizes they cannot escape judgment.

Click to expand

Etymology

Baldur (Baldr) — "Bold, Brave" or "Shining." Perhaps born after the Crossing, never touched by the old sins.

His Death

All things swore not to harm him except the mistletoe—too young, too innocent to swear. Killed by a mistletoe dart, guided by Loki, thrown by blind Hodr. He waits in Hel until after Ragnarok—then he will return to rule the renewed world.

In Our Story

Gentle, wondering, sees beauty in everything, cannot comprehend malice. His death is the turning point—the moment the Nephilim of Asgard understand that their refuge is not permanent. Judgment finds everyone eventually.

Heimdall The Watchman

"World-Brightener" — Guardian of the Rainbow Bridge

Born of NINE MOTHERS—waves of the sea. Created, not born naturally. He can hear grass grow. He watched the Flood from Asgard's cliffs. He heard every scream.

Click to expand

Etymology

Heimdall (Heimdallr) — "World-Brightener" or "World's Pillar." He illuminates the boundary between realms.

His Role

  • Guardian of Bifrost—the Rainbow Bridge, a frequency pathway between realms
  • Gjallarhorn — His horn will announce Ragnarok, heard in all nine realms
  • Golden teeth—his body was enhanced, modified
  • Can hear grass grow, wool grow on sheep, needs less sleep than a bird

In Our Story

Long detailed sentences—he notices everything. Calm, patient, faithful, lonely. He watched the Flood from Asgard's cliffs. He heard every scream across the waters. He never speaks of it. He never forgets.

Loki The Trickster

Etymology Uncertain — The Shapeshifter Among Gods

NOT Nephilim—a Jotun (giant) who lives among the gods. Or the Silence itself given form. Blood-brother to Odin. Father of monsters. The question Asgard refuses to answer.

Click to expand

Etymology

Loki — Etymology uncertain. Possibly "to close/lock," "flame," or "to bind." No fixed meaning because he has no fixed nature.

His Children

  • Fenrir — The wolf bound by Tyr's sacrifice
  • Jormungandr — The World Serpent encircling Midgard
  • Hel — Goddess of the dead, ruler of the realm of the same name

The Question

Is Loki a Nephilim who turned to the Silence? Is he the Silence itself, wearing flesh? Or something else entirely? He has no fixed form because he has no true nature— frequency-instability made manifest. They invited the enemy inside.

His Fate

He caused Baldur's death. Now bound with his son's entrails, serpent venom dripping on his face. His writhing causes earthquakes. He will break free at Ragnarok. Quick, clever, charming—he says truths people prefer not to hear.
